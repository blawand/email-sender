How to Use OmniSend
Choose Your Mode:
  • Send using CSV Data: Best for sending personalized emails based on data in a spreadsheet (CSV file).
  • Manual Recipient List: Best for sending the exact same email to a list of people you type or paste in. No personalization from external data.
CSV Mode Steps:
  1. Upload CSV: Select your `.csv` file. The first row MUST contain headers (e.g., `Email`, `FirstName`, `ProductName`). Header names are case-sensitive.
  2. Detected Headers: Check the list of headers found in your file. These are your available placeholders.
  3. Recipient Template: Enter the placeholder that matches your email address column (e.g., `{Email}`). ONLY ONE placeholder allowed here.
  4. Compose Email:
    • Subject/Body: Write your content. Use the buttons or the icon in the editor toolbar to insert placeholders like `{FirstName}` or `{ProductName}` where needed. They MUST exactly match the detected headers (case-sensitive).
    • Attachments (Optional): Add files to attach to every email.
  5. Preview: Click "Generate Preview" to see how the email looks using data from the *first* row of your CSV. Check if placeholders are replaced correctly.
  6. Send: Click "Send Emails". You might be asked to log in and authorize with Google the first time or if your session expires (check for pop-ups/new tabs).
Manual Mode Steps:
  1. Enter Recipients: Type or paste email addresses into the text box. Separate them with commas, spaces, or new lines.
  2. Compose Email: Write your Subject and Body. Add any Attachments. Important: Any text like `{Placeholder}` will be sent *exactly* as written; it will NOT be replaced with data. Do not use the placeholder inserter buttons in this mode unless you intend to send literal `{Placeholder}` text.
  3. Preview: Click "Generate Preview" to see the static email content.
  4. Send: Click "Send Emails". You may need to authorize with Google.
General Notes:
  • Authentication: Uses Google OAuth2 to send emails via your Gmail account. Your Google password is never stored or seen by this app.
  • Placeholders (CSV Mode): Must be enclosed in curly braces `{}` and match CSV headers exactly (case-sensitive).
  • Rate Limits: Gmail has sending limits. Sending large batches too quickly may result in temporary blocks or errors.
  • Error Handling: Check the status messages and results list after sending for any issues.
